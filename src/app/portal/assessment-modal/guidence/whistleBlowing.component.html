Relevant to:

All Suppliers

Background:

Banks will want to ensure that’s a minimum, suppliers are reporting concerns about risk, suspected malpractice/wrongdoing within the suppliers firm that has an impact on the bank and/or its customers

Regulators:

FCA

Aligns to the legislation and/ or regulations:

Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998
Financial Conduct Authority Handbook (SYSC) Chapter 18 and Policy Statement 15/24 Whistleblowing in deposit-takers and, PRA-designated investment firms and insurers Regulation.

Recommended components required to support your competence:

Procedures:

Title:
Reporting suspected wrongdoing
Details:
The supplier’s management may be required to encourage workers to Report if they suspect or witness malpractice/wrongdoing, relating to services provided to the Bank, either through annual training or refresher communications. This may be required to include details of how and where they can report concerns.
Title:
Reporting suspected wrongdoing – new workers
Details:
The supplier may be required to ensure new workers are made aware that if they suspect or witness malpractice/wrongdoing, relating to services provided to the Bank
Title:
All relevant investigations are reported.
Details:
When investigating a Report concern that has an impact on the bank and/or its customers, the supplier may be required to ensure the Bank. The supplier may be required to ensure that information contained in the investigation report is accurate and up-to-date
