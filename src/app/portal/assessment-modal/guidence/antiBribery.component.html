Relevant to:

All Suppliers

Background:

Suppliers are likely to be required to ensure that they, nor anyone engaged by the Supplier to act for or on behalf of a Bank, may be required to not: a. offer, promise, give, request, agree to receive or accept a bribe; or b. do anything to circumvent controls in place to deter, prevent or detect bribery
An Anti Bribery Policy is required to assist in managing the risk of payments, offers, promises of a bribe (making a bribe) or receipt, requests or acceptance of a bribe (being bribed) by an individual or associated party. Suppliers should develop their procedures with the aim of mitigating the following failings:
  • comply with anti-bribery legislation both in the UK and in the other jurisdiction
  • maintain consistent, proportionate and effective approach to anti- bribery;
  • respond effectively to 'an offer or acceptance of any gift, fee, reward or other advantage to or from any person as an inducement to do something improper, which is dishonest, illegal or a breach of trust’. This includes offers and promises to give as well as requests and agreements to receive bribes.
Reasonable steps outlined by the FCA include:
  1. senior management that stays up to date with, and fully abreast of, bribery and corruption issues (Top Level Commitment)
  2. adequate bribery and corruption risk assessment
  3. policies and procedures (including staff recruitment, vetting and remuneration) that cover bribery and corruption risks
  4. training and awareness programmes that ensure staff have adequate understanding of the risks associated with bribery and corruption
  5. adequate and risk-sensitive measures that address the risk that a third party acting on behalf of the firm may engage in corruption It is advised that in order to comply, suppliers shall align with legislative requirements and to the core principles contained in the UK Ministry of Justice Guidance issued under Section 9 of the UK Bribery Act 2010.
Top Level Commitment

Corruption is defined as the abuse of entrusted power for private gain and is a term used to describe a wide range of financial misconduct. Banks are likely to take a Zero-Tolerance approach for this topic. Any incidents or potential incidents of corruption by the Supplier should be reported in accordance with any related bank specific policies that deal with activities that fall under the definition of corruption.

Top Level Commitment

Effective top level commitment allows an organisation to drive an appropriate and effective anti-bribery culture. Suppliers may consider appointing a nominated individual(s) with technical expertise and professional credibility to be responsible for the management of anti-bribery risk

Recommended components required to support your competence:

Training:

We recommend that you are able to demonstrate that you have suitable training material and plans in place and are able to provide current reports on the number of your staff who have been trained, demonstrating that training takes place as regularly as required by any legislation
Title:
Anti Bribery Training for all staff
Details:
Specific training related to both staff individual roles and the level of bribery risk associated with the role.

Procedures:

Banks will want to see that there is a relevant an appropriate Record keeping and governance approach where procedures are required.You should be able to demonstrate that your company is set up to record all items listed and ensure appropriate escalation and improvement process is in place
Title:
Threat and Risk Assessments
Details:
The purpose of a risk assessment is to allow the supplier to identify and mitigate bribery risks associated with the conduct of their business. The supplier may be required to undertake a regular anti-bribery risk assessment in order to identify and mitigate the bribery risks associated with the conduct of its business. The supplier may be required to ensure that all risks identified are managed and mitigated by the application of its anti-bribery controls.
Title:
Risk Based Anti Bribery Due Diligence Report
Details:
The purpose of due diligence is to identify and mitigate the risk that a bribe is offered to obtain or retain business relating to the bank, or an advantage in the conduct of the bank or the supplier’s business.
Guidance:
The supplier may be required to complete risk-based anti-bribery due diligence on persons (including individuals and incorporated or unincorporated bodies) who will perform services for or on behalf of the bank which is appropriate and relevant to such services. Further guidance on risk-based due diligence can be obtained from your supplier manager if required. In determining whether a person is acting for or on behalf of the bank, the supplier may be required to consider the nature of the activity being undertaken. As a minimum, an agent, subsidiary or any other person obtaining, retaining or conducting business on behalf of the bank may be required to be subject to due diligence. The supplier may be required to, as part of their due diligence activities, identify all directors and perform appropriate additional checks to ascertain whether or not any of the directors are public officials and/or politically exposed persons (PEPs). Where the nature of the services provided and the involvement of public officials and/or PEPs leads to an increase in bribery risks, further guidance may be required to be sought from your supplier manager.
Title:
Ongoing Assessment of procedures and processes
Details:
The supplier may be required to ensure that arrangements are in place to monitor regularly the continued adequacy and effectiveness of processes, systems and controls in order to assess compliance with this document. This will include any assurance, oversight and/or audit reviews.
Title:
Gifts and Entertainment Register and Assessment and escalation
Details:
The supplier may be required to ensure that they do not offer, either personally or on behalf of the Bank, gifts, entertainment and hospitality (GEH) intended to unduly influence a decision-maker, supplier or customer in order to gain or retain business with or on behalf of the Bank.
Guidance:
You should ensure that you maintain records of all GEH and to demonstrate that they are proportionate and relevant to both an individual’s role and the relationship between the supplier and the offeror or recipient of the GEH.
Title:
Charitable and political donations
Details:
The supplier is likely to be require to NOT make any Charitabke or political donation.
Title:
Facilitation Payments
Details:
A facilitation payment is a payment which is made to expedite or secure the performance of a routine non-discretionary action, such as processing papers, issuing permits, and other actions by a person which they are already bound to perform. These are illegal in the UK
Guidance:
The supplier shall ensure appropriate processes and reporting mechanisms are in place as well as suitable risk mitigation procedures.
Title:
High Risk Third Parties
Details:
Where employees are identified as working in roles considered high risk for bribery risks role specific training should be considered to ensure that employees are aware as to the increased bribery risks associated with their roles.
