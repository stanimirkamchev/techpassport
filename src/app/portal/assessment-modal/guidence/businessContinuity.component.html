Background:

A business continuity plan (BCP) is a plan to help ensure that business processes can continue during a time of emergency or disaster. Such emergencies or disasters might include a fire or any other case where business is not able to occur under normal conditions. It is best practice for every organisation to develop a BCP to ensure its business operations can continue, no matter the disruption. A critical component of that business plan is to ensure that users remain productive while maintaining the necessary level of security and control over user access to corporate resource Banks will want to ensure that suppliers have adequate and effective resilience and recovery strategies or business continuity systems and control in the place to prevent the following (not limited to):
  • Failure to effectively identify and classify business processes, their end to end dependencies and to plan, prepare and implement an effective Business Continuity strategy and response framework for significant incidents
  • Failure to respond effectively to significant incidents
  • Failure to assess the effectiveness of Business Continuity strategy and readiness to respond to significant incidents
  • Failure to review and update the response framework for significant incidents
  • Failure to maintain an effective and resilient end to end control environment for Critical Business Processes (CBPs).
  • Failure to effectively prepare, respond and learn from Building Incidents.

The Supplier may be required to establish a Resilience & Continuity policy, provides a framework for setting Resilience & Continuity objectives and defines the standards for their implementation and operation.

The Supplier may be required to appoint a person, to be accountable for implementation of the policy, monitoring the Key Controls & Indicators defined below.

Potential considerations in your policy may be required to focus on the following 4 themes: Operational Resilience, Business Continuity Management, IT Disaster Recovery and Incident Response.

Title:
Operations Resilience Structure
Details:
Suppliers who are critical to the delivery of the CBPs Are likely to be required to meet the following requirements: (not limited to)
  • The Supplier may be required to provide sign off on an annual basis to the respective bank Supplier Manager that the service can be met and understand the role they play in the Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of the CBP.
  • The Supplier may be required to review their bank contractual agreements on a 12 monthly basis with Supplier Manager to ensure it remains up to date and fit for purpose.
  • The Supplier may be required to provide confirmation that any changes made to the contractual agreements by bank are understood & embedded within the agreed time scales set by the Supplier Manager.
  • The Supplier may be required to comply with any annual assurance undertaken by bank. Issues identified as a result of the assurance may be required to have appropriate action plans in place with defined dates for action closure.
  • The Supplier may be required to define and document the roles and responsibilities of all key person dependencies that underpin the service supporting the bank CBP.
  • The Supplier may be required to ensure that key staff supporting the CBP service are aware of their roles & responsibilities in relation to the service supporting the bank CBP on a minimum 12 monthly basis through inductions or training. This may be evidenced by the maintenance of a local induction/training log for key staff.
  • The Supplier may be required to identify 4th party Suppliers that are critical to the delivery of the service supporting the bank CBP and may be required to evidence their ability to meet the CBP Recovery Time Objective (RTO) & Recovery Time Capability (RTC). Any deficiencies/risks may be required to be documented, and actioned where necessary in line with risk appetite.
  • The Supplier may be required to identify and document those applications/systems that are critical to the delivery of their service supporting the bank CBP.
  • The Supplier may be required to have appropriate plans in place to manage cyber attacks relating to Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the service supporting the bank CBP.
  • The Supplier may be required to ensure that there are no Single Point/s of Failure (SPOF) in relation to key person dependencies as part of the service supporting the bank CBP.
  • The Supplier may be required to ensure that details of key person dependencies, BUs and their continuity arrangements are detailed in the appropriate Business Continuity Plan.
  • The Supplier may be required to ensure that, as a minimum, cross site capability is in place for those services provided in support of an bank CBP.
Guidance:
  1. Critical CBP Suppliers may be required to confirm and evidence their capability to meet CBP RTO requirements. This includes confirmation that:
    1. roles and responsibilities in relation to key person CBP dependencies are defined and documented
    2. all applications/systems are critical to the delivery of their service have been identified and documented
    3. no Single Point/s of Failure (SPOF) in relation to key person dependencies have been identified as part of the service
    4. there is cross site capability for those services provided in support of the bank CBP
  2. Critical CBP Suppliers may be required to provide evidence they have appropriate plans in place to manage cyber attacks relating to Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the service supporting the Bank’s CBP.
